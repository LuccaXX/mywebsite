Equilibrium

Yin Yang is a fundamental concept in Chinese philosophy and the Tao religion. It represents the idea that opposite forces are interconnected and interdependent in the natural world.

These forces are not in conflict but rather complement each other, creating balance and harmony. Inspired by the well-known Yin Yang symbol, I wanted to represent these principles using ink and water. The black ink bleeds into the white and vice versa, indicating that within each force, there is always a hint of the other.

Inspired by the contrast between desert and tundra, the second installation portrays how this concept of Yin and Yang is deeply reflected in *Game of Thrones.Between Jon Snow and Daenerys Targaryen. Their characters, though seemingly opposite, share an intrinsic connection—one that mirrors the idea of balance and interdependence.

Jon Snow embodies the cold, the night, the passive and steady force. Raised in the icy North, he is introspective, guided by duty, and often reluctant to seek power. Like Yin, which is receptive and grounding, Jon is a stabilizing force, enduring hardships and making sacrifices for the greater good.

His leadership is defined by humility, restraint, and an unwavering sense of honor. Daenerys Targaryen, on the other hand, represents Yang—the fire, the sun, the active and dynamic force. Born in exile, she is driven by passion, ambition, and an unyielding desire for change. Like Yang, which is assertive and commanding, Daenerys is a force of movement and transformation, igniting revolutions and reshaping the world in her vision. Her leadership is fueled by vision, charisma, and an almost divine sense of destiny. Yet, despite their stark differences, Jon and Daenerys are not purely Yin or Yang. Jon, though a man of ice, carries fire within him in his moments of fierce leadership.

Daenerys, though a woman of fire, holds ice in her heart. A vulnerability that mirrors Jon’s own struggles. Their personalities blend into each other. I wanted to portray that this interplay is not only seen between the characters but within a single individual as well. Every person holds both forces within them—moments of uiet reflection and moments of bold action, kindness and ruthlessness, restraint and impulse.

True balance comes from embracing both sides rather than rejecting one in favour of the other. You would be incomplete without one or the other, harmony exists in embracing both.

The above quotes come from the Tao Te Ching which can be translated as The Book of the Immanence of the Way or The Book of the Way and How It Manifests itself in the World or simply The Book of the Way. Inspired by these quotes, the final installation is a text composition that emphasises the idea that true fulfilment comes from embracing seeming contradictions.
